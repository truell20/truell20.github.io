Michael Truell

Hello. I am a high school student at the Horace Mann School in New York. I have programmed for 4+ years in C++, Java, Android, Python, Javascript, and the LAMP stack. I also have much experience with machine learning (see Fido).

Projects

  • Fido - A universal robotic control system using reinforcement learning with limited feedback. Being researched for the New York City Science and Engineering Fair 2016 and the Science Research class at Horace Mann School. The code is currently private. However, the paper and results are public.
  • Homer 2.0 - a program that uses Recurrent Neural Networks equipped with Long-Short Term Memory to write in the style of the ancient poet Homer.
  • New York Computer Science League - we host algorithmic competitions for select New York City High Schools. Winner of the Def Hacks Hackathon hosted by the Stuyvesant High School. Endorsed by the Horace Mann Schoo. Expected release is January 2016.
  • Halite - a game played by AIs equipped with a lightweight library for writing AIs in Java, C++, and Python.
  • ApPosition - A scheduling and location app for the Horace Mann School.
  • ACTHelper - an interactive and personalized way to prepare for the ACT. First place winner of HackMann Spring 2015.
  • Circle - a minimalistic 2D multiplayer shooter.
  • Cheatr - an iOS app that allowed the faking of high scores for over 25 unique mobile games and utilized a LAMP backend.
  • White Tile Cheat - an Android app that allowed the faking of high scores for the game Don't Tap the White Tile. The app received over 80,000+ downloads.
